--- layout: template title: struts2 internationalize ---

step1

open struts.xml, add following contents

<!-- specify the base name of global international resources -->
<constant name="struts.custom.i18n.resources" value="message" />
<!-- specify charset of international encoding-->
<constant name="struts.i18n.encoding" value="UTF-8" />

step2

add message_zh_CN.properties and message_en_US.properties under source folder,the rule of resource file name is "basename+language+country.properties"

message_en_US.properties

appname=ofos
home=home
login=login
logout=logout
register=register
cart=cart
personal_center=personal center

message_zh_CN.properties

appname=\u8BA2\u9910\u7CFB\u7EDF
home=\u4E3B\u9875
login=\u767B\u5F55
logout=\u6CE8\u9500
register=\u6CE8\u518C
cart=\u8D2D\u7269\u8F66
personal_center=\u4E2A\u4EBA\u4E2D\u5FC3

step3

use struts2 tag in jsp file,for example

<title><s:text name="appname"></s:text></title>

you can change the default language of IE to see whether your international resources take effect
